In the world of software engineering, obstacle are bound to come up. When solving a problem, sometimes you do not know what to do, or you get stuck because of an error or lack of knowledge. For me, I would first review the problem and code to see if I can determine my next step. Like many, if I am stuck and could not find the solution, I would ask others or the internet. We would not always know the answers to every problem, so communication is a vital skill that software engineers should have. Currently, there are active communities online willing to assist. However, the format of how you ask a question can affect the answer and attitudes of others. In an article, How To Ask Questions The Smart Way by Eric Steven Raymond discusses guidelines that can help determine whether is question is a “smart way”, or “not smart way.”
 
## Smart Way
A “smart way” of asking questions according to Raymond are those who tried searching for help from other forums, websites, or videos. By stating what resources that did not work, it narrows down the possibilities of those that would work. Also, questions that ask for assistance and guidance instead of answers is a “smart way” of asking questions. This format promotes that you want to learn and gain knowledge. An example of a “smart way” of asking questions is this user who posted a question on stack overflow about, “How do I break out of nested loops in Java”. In the users' post, it states that this person tried looking at similar questions, but none were about java. The user explains what they are looking for and provided an easy to read example code. This post was published 11 years ago, and now it has over 1.2 million views. Showing that by asking a question the “smart way”, it may help others who also have a similar question find answers. This format also led to 35 answers, many showing different ways of answering this question or adding on to someone’s reply.
 
Here is a link to [Stackoverflow: “How do I break out of nested loops in Java”](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/886955/how-do-i-break-out-of-nested-loops-in-java).
## Not Smart Way
A “Not smart way” of asking questions according to Raymond are those that just ask for the answer and not willing to think for themselves. It gives the impression that you are only interested in the answer and not the steps leading to the answer. Also, small formats and grammar such as putting “please help me!” or “urgent!” is a “not smart way” of asking questions. An example of a “Not smart way” of asking a question is this user who posted on stack overflow, titled “I want add the number from left to right in an array. The answer should be only one number”. There are small grammar errors in the title and description. This user just asked the question and posted his/her code. The responses were the problems of the code and state that they do not want to do their homework for them completely. The responses are helpful but there are too many errors to solve. This post shows a lack of effort and interest. Stack overflow now gives an option for other users to improve this question.
