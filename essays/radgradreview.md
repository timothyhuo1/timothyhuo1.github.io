Deciding what you want to pursue for the rest of your life is a difficult choice to make. Going into college, I wasn’t too sure what computer science was. Luckily, I was introduced to RadGrad during my first semester of college. RadGrad provides a platform to help individuals find and develop their interest in computer science. It presents an alternative to the basic GPA with ICE (Innovation, Competency, Experience). These components help shape an individual to become a more well-rounded computer science student. RadGrad is still an ongoing project I will be going over the strengths and weaknesses of the current version of RadGrad.  

##Strengths

#To-Do List
RadGrad does a great job of breaking down the functionality of the website so you aren’t overwhelmed. I think the Personal To-Do List is a very helpful feature, especially for those who are first using the site. It organizes all the tasks into groups depending on priority and provides a good indication of what is needed to be reviewed again. When I was addressing the high and medium priorities, it showed me my current information without having to visit that page directly. For example, my Career Goals had my current ones being displayed on the card and it asked me to review them since I haven’t within the past three months. I think the system reminding you to review and update certain things past a time interval is amazing since my career goals for example could have changed. And this change can impact other parts of the page for the better like my recommended opportunities. Everything is laid out for you in a compact format and it only takes a few clicks to explore what RadGrad has to offer. 

#Relationships Between Interest, Courses, and Opportunities
In the field of Computer Science, there are so many areas of interest and opportunities that come with them. RadGrad helps minimize the amount of effort to finding opportunities relevant to interest or course. The design of the Interests and Courses page not only tells the user what this certain section is about but also all the related opportunities. This is also reflected in the opportunities page, wherein each opportunity shows all the related courses and interests. These features can be used to plan out what you want to pursue and have a more enjoyable experience in college. I was looking through the Opportunities page and there were so many to choose from. At first, it may seem difficult to find a certain opportunity that the user might be interested in, but RadGrad solves this by adding options to filter and sort by certain requirements. Instead of looking through the whole list of opportunities, these filters allow users to narrow down their choices. One filter that I found interesting was the Recommended, where it filters all the opportunities depending on my existing information.     

#A Scale for Success
RadGrad empathizes that GPA is not the only factor that is important to a computer science student. They implemented a system called ICE, which measures innovation, competency, and experience from not only courses but also opportunities. I feel that this system leads computer science students in a better direction. When trying to find a job, the experience and what you have done play a huge role in the hiring process. This system encouraged me to do other extracurricular activities and try to explore what the community has to offer. 

##Weaknesses

#Planner Page
For the Planner page, it seems like everything in the planner is so close together and I had trouble trying to drag the correct opportunity/course to a different semester. It is a bit difficult to hover over the opportunity/course when trying to show the indication of what the class is or how many points is that opportunity. For the right side, it seems like the opportunities and courses that you favored just keeps extending down the page, I think having a scroll here would be a better implementation. 

Errors:
- There are duplicate courses 
- For some courses, it shows 0 for all innovation, competency, and experience. 

#Blank Spaces
The formatting of a page when there is a lot of tags can create a lot of blank spaces. The example in the image above shows the course page to introduction to Programming for the Web (ICS 415). In the Not In Plan section on the left side, there is a list of opportunities that are related to this course. However, this leaves a huge blank space on the right side because there are no course reviews. Even if there were course reviews, the Not In Plan tags will overwhelm the other sections on that left side. I think adding a dropdown or view more button will minimize the page length and space.

#Organization
When users check off certain visibility control, this is reflected in the interests and career pages. For example in the image above, shows certain student tags standing out, like having an image or showing their radgrad level. I feel like this should consistent and only have a name and last name. Another thing when you check off courses in the visibility control, I think the courses should be sorted by course number, currently, it doesn’t seem to be in any order.

##Improvements
- A nice addition to filters on the opportunities page would be searching by interests or even name. This allows users to display only what they want, similar to the recommended filter but more direct. 
- Looking at the navbar, some pages could be combined to shorten the length of the navbar. Such as, combining the ICE and Levels pages or creating a dropdown feature for them. 
- RadGrad has a lot of pages and great functionality that new users might overlook. I think having an assist feature will be helpful. For example, having a button that creates small popups next to the different pages on the navbar, showing what it is, and a click event that will move to the next popup.
