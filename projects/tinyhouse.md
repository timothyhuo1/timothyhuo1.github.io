In my senior year of high school, our engineer tech II class participated in the 2019 SSFM Tiny House Design Challenge. This was a local design challenge, focusing on Hawaii as cost of living is high and space is limited. We were tasked to design and build a tiny house with a theme of sustainability. As a class, we all came up with designs using sketchup and chose one to continue and build. It was designed as a studio apartment, aiming towards college students and young adults. 

The group that was chosen had to come up with a budget sheet, portfolio, and presentation. The rest of the class worked on the construction. My task in this project was to build the tiny house and help with the presentation. Proffessionals in the industry came to help us build and they taught us how to plan and exacute. This was my first hands-on project, I learned how to operate tools and tricks in construction. 

For this Tiny House Design Challenge, we had to follow a schedule or we wouldn't have been able to participate. We were broken into groups, those who prepare the pieces and those who constructed the tiny house. We had a whole class building this tiny house but only a couple of tools we could work with, so communication amongs the groups were important. This experience helped me realize the significance of working together and being patient in working with a group.
